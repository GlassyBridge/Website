The Mysterious Comets

Icy bodies that release gas and dust as they approach the Sun.

Understanding Comets

Comets are cosmic snowballs of frozen gases, rock, and dust that orbit the Sun. When frozen, they are typically only a few kilometers across. However, as a comet's orbit brings it close to the Sun, the heat vaporizes its icy nucleus, creating a visible atmosphere or coma and sometimes a tail of gas and/or dust.

Parts of a Comet

  • Nucleus: The solid, central part of a comet.
  • Coma: A hazy atmosphere surrounding the nucleus, formed when ice vaporizes.
  • Tail: A long stream of gas and dust blown away from the Sun by solar wind and radiation pressure. Comets can have both a gas tail (ion tail) and a dust tail.

Famous Comets

Some well-known comets include Halley's Comet, Comet Hale-Bopp, and Comet NEOWISE.
