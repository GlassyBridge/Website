The Sun: Our Radiant Star

The heart of our solar system, a giant ball of hot plasma.

Overview of the Sun

The Sun is a G-type main-sequence star that contains about 99.86% of the total mass of the Solar System. Its gravity dominates the system, keeping all the planets, dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and other small bodies in orbit. The Sun's energy, in the form of sunlight and heat, sustains almost all life on Earth through photosynthesis and drives Earth's climate and weather.

Structure

  • Core: The Sun's central region where nuclear fusion occurs, converting hydrogen to helium and releasing immense energy.
  • Radiative Zone: Energy from the core travels outwards through radiation.
  • Convective Zone: Hotter plasma rises and cooler plasma sinks, transferring energy towards the surface.
  • Photosphere: The visible surface of the Sun.
  • Chromosphere: A layer above the photosphere, characterized by its reddish glow.
  • Corona: The outermost layer of the Sun's atmosphere, extending millions of kilometers into space.

Fun Fact

Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth!
