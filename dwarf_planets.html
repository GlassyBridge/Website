The Realm of Dwarf Planets

Exploring the smaller, fascinating celestial bodies in our solar system.

What is a Dwarf Planet?

A dwarf planet is a celestial body that orbits the Sun and has enough mass to be rounded by its own gravity but has not cleared its orbital neighborhood of other objects. This distinguishes them from the eight "classical" planets.

Notable Dwarf Planets

  • Pluto: Once considered the ninth planet, Pluto is the most famous dwarf planet, located in the Kuiper Belt.
  • Ceres: The largest object in the asteroid belt, located between Mars and Jupiter.
  • Eris: A trans-Neptunian object even more massive than Pluto, located in the scattered disk.
  • Haumea: A rapidly spinning, elongated dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt.
  • Makemake: Another large Kuiper Belt object, known for its reddish color.
